Research Background

Why This Research?

1

The expanded enforcement of the Serious Accidents Punishment Act in 2024 has significantly increased demand for qualified safety managers at construction sites. Although the government introduced a Shared Safety Manager Support Program to address this gap, the supply of safety personnel remains insufficient to meet the growing need.

2

While safety management technologies using BIM and machine learning have been developed both domestically and internationally, they are largely limited to image- and sensor-based hazard detection or predictive risk assessment, falling short of supporting the full scope of on-site decision-making.

3

Emerging generative AI-based safety management tools have also failed to adequately reflect the actual workflows and logical judgment processes of human safety managers, limiting their practical applicability in real construction environments.

Research Objective

What We Aim to Build

This research aims to develop a Cognitive Agent System (CAS) for automating and advancing safety management at construction sites. The system integrates three core components — a Dynamic Ontology Foundry, a Construction Safety Knowledge DB, and a Multimodal AI Model — into a unified cognitive framework.

By coupling this system with an autonomous adaptive learning module and an AI–human collaboration framework, the research seeks to enable real-time risk prediction, automated generation of response measures, and a sophisticated safety management agent that reflects the actual logic of expert decision-making.
